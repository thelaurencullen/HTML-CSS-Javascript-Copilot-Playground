Low FODMAP Vegan Pasta Recipe

This pasta dish features gluten-free pasta tossed in a flavorful, low FODMAP tomato and spinach sauce. It's enhanced with sliced olives and a blend of low FODMAP spices like dried basil or oregano, then garnished with fresh parsley. It's a delicious, tummy-friendly, and vegan-friendly meal option.

Ingredients

  • 8 ounces (about 2 cups) of gluten-free pasta (look for pasta made from rice, corn, or another low FODMAP grain)
  • 2 tablespoons garlic-infused olive oil (make sure it's infused, not just flavored)
  • 1 can (14 ounces) diced tomatoes (or 2 cups of fresh diced tomatoes)
  • 1 cup spinach leaves, washed and chopped
  • 1/2 cup pitted black olives, sliced
  • 2 tablespoons fresh parsley, chopped
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • Red pepper flakes (optional, for some heat)
  • Low FODMAP spices (e.g., dried basil, dried oregano, or dried thyme), to taste
  • Vegan Parmesan cheese or nutritional yeast (optional, for serving)

Instructions

  1. Cook the Gluten-Free Pasta:
    • Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add the gluten-free pasta and cook according to the package instructions until al dente. Drain and set aside.
  2. Sauté the Tomatoes and Spinach:
    • In a large skillet, heat the garlic-infused olive oil over medium heat.
    • Add the diced tomatoes (canned or fresh) and let them simmer for about 5 minutes until they start to break down and release their juices.
    • Stir in the chopped spinach and cook for an additional 2-3 minutes until the spinach wilts.
  3. Add Olives, Spices, and Parsley:
    • Stir in the sliced olives and add your choice of low FODMAP spices (e.g., dried basil, dried oregano, or dried thyme) to taste. Start with 1/2 to 1 teaspoon of each, depending on your preference.
    • Continue to cook for another 2 minutes, allowing the olives and spices to blend with the sauce.
    • Season the sauce with salt, pepper, and red pepper flakes (if using). Adjust the seasoning to your taste.
  4. Combine with Gluten-Free Pasta:
    • Add the cooked and drained gluten-free pasta to the skillet with the tomato and spinach mixture. Toss everything together to coat the pasta evenly with the sauce. Cook for an additional 2 minutes to heat the pasta through.
  5. Serve:
    • Divide the pasta into serving plates or bowls.
    • Garnish with extra chopped parsley and vegan Parmesan cheese or nutritional yeast if desired.

By adding low FODMAP spices like dried basil, dried oregano, or dried thyme, you'll enhance the flavor of this dish while keeping it suitable for a low FODMAP diet. Enjoy your flavorful and tummy-friendly meal!
